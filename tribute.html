Swami Vivekananda

(1863 - 1902)



Biography

  1. Swami Vivekananda was an Indian philosopher, a Hindu monk and a spiritual leader.

  2. His birth name was Narendranath Datta, which later changed to Swami Vivekananda.

  3. He was born on January 12 in 1863.

  4. He belonged to a Bengali family from Kolkata.

  5. He was a disciple of Shri Ramakrishna Paramhansa.

  6. His birthday is celebrated as National Youth Day in India.

  7. He gave a memorable speech in Chicago in 1893.

  8. He is the founder of the Ramakrishna Mission.

  9. He spent his life teaching people about Vedanta.

  10. He died on the 4th of July in 1902 at Belur Math while meditating.

Teachings and Philosophy

  1. Swami Vivekananda advocated for religious tolerance and harmony, asserting that all religions lead to the same ultimate truth.

  2. He believed that understanding and respecting different religious paths can foster unity and peace among people of diverse faiths.

  3. Vivekananda emphasized the power of individual effort and willpower in achieving success and personal transformation.

  4. He encouraged individuals to integrate spiritual ideals such as compassion, self-discipline, and selfless service into their actions and interactions with others.

Legacy and Influence

  1. Vivekananda's message resonates strongly with the youth, inspiring them to strive for personal excellence, moral integrity, and social responsibility.

  2. Swami Vivekananda's emphasis on the unity of religions and the acceptance of diverse spiritual paths has significantly contributed to interfaith dialogue.

  3. His speech on religious tolerance and the universality of all religions garnered immense attention, making him a prominent figure and opening doors for the study and practice of Vedanta outside of India.

  4. Vivekananda's participation in the World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago in 1893 brought the philosophy of Vedanta to the Western world.

Quotes and Inspirational Messages

  1. "Strength is life, weakness is death. Expansion is life, contraction is death. Love is life, hatred is death."

  2. "In a day, when you don't come across any problems, you can be sure that you are traveling in a wrong path."

  3. "Arise, awake, and stop not till the goal is reached."

  4. "Take up one idea. Make that one idea your life - think of it, dream of it, live on that idea. Let the brain, muscles, nerves, every part of your body be full of that idea, and just leave every other idea alone. This is the way to success."
